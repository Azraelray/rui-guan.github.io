CV (7/2021)   

Chenfanfu Jiang is an assistant professor of Mathematics at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA).

Jiang was an assistant professor of Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania 2017-2021. He received the PhD degree (`15) from UCLA co-advised by Demetri Terzopoulos and Joseph Teran. He was as a Postdoctoral Scholar at UCLA 2015-2017. He completed a B.S. in Physics (`10) from the Special Class for the Gifted Young (SCGY) at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC).

Jiang is a recipient of the UCLA Edward K. Rice Outstanding Doctoral Student Award (2015), NSF CRII award (2018) and NSF CAREER award (2020). His current research interests include physics-based modeling/simulation and scientific computing. He works on numerical algorithms facilitating advancements in computer graphics, computational physics, and inverse problems (mainly in structural mechanics, vision, and robotics).
